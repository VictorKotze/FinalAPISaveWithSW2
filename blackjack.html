History of BlackJack

The origin of BlackJack is not known, but historians agree that it most likely originated from French Casino in 1700 where it was called "Vingt-et-Un", meaning "Twenty-one."
Blackjack mainly become popular in the United States from the 1820s and was called 21 mainly from Nevada.

The name "BlackJack" came from casino's trying to make the game more popular through benefits. They offered bonus payouts, including one that paid extra if a blackjack (a jack of spades or clubs) was dealt along with an ace of spades.

How to play

The goal in BlackJack is to get your overall card worth closer than 21 than your enemy. The person who is the nearest to 21 wins, but don't go over 21 because you lose. you can still while being over 21 if your enemy gets a very low number not close to 21.
To pick up a card press the "Hit" button, when you think you have enough cards press "Stand" to make the enemy pick up it's cards. If you press "Stand" you won't be able to pick up any more cards for your round. If you go over 21 your turn will automatically end and the enemy will play.

Card values, and special card Values

Each card is worth their number such as an 6 of hearts being worth 6 points, except special Cards.

Some card in the game have special values, these include:
King: 10 Points
Queen: 10 Points
Jack: 10 Points
Ace: 11 Points
